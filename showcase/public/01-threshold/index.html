Threshold

A museum of light

Four rooms. No objects. The collection is the air itself — tuned, colored, and set breathing. Nothing here is behind glass, because nothing here is a thing.

01

Room I

Meridian

Vera Lindqvist

Light, duration — 2017

Lindqvist raises a horizon that belongs to no landscape. The room holds the last minute before a sunrise that has been indefinitely postponed. Visitors tend to wait; the room, on principle, does not.

02

Room II

Vespers

Tomás Abreu

Light, breath — 2019

An oval of evening opens in the far wall, or appears to. Abreu tuned its swelling to the pulse of a resting adult, so the aperture breathes at the pace of someone nearly asleep. Stand close enough and you will begin to match it.

03

Room III

Static

Hana Okabe

Light, patience — 2021

The brightest room in the museum and the hardest one to see. Okabe set the white a few degrees off neutral and lets it wander; the drift becomes visible about ninety seconds after you stop looking for it.

04

Room IV

Undertow

Elias Vann

Light, weight — 2024

Light enters at the ceiling and never reaches the floor. Vann calls the piece a study in generous loss: the blue deepens the way a held breath does — past comfort, into calm, and a little past that.

Colophon

Threshold is a fictional museum imagined for this showcase. Its rooms are rendered live in a single WebGL fragment shader — no photographs, no video, no images of any kind. The light you just walked through is mathematics, breathing.
