II · Les Accords

Three accords,
folded into one smoke

Fumée Nº 1 is composed to be discovered the way night discovers a room — gradually, and then all at once.

I · Tête

Braise froide

cold ember

The strike of a match already gone out. Birch tar and pink pepper lifted from a cold hearth — bright for a single breath, then silver, then memory.

II · Cœur

Racine d'iris

iris root

Orris butter aged six years in darkness. Powder and suede, the hush of a glove drawn off, the inside of a violin case closed at midnight.

III · Fond

Thé noir

black tea

Lapsang leaves smoked over pine, worn leather, a drop of dark honey. The last warm thing in the room, and the longest to leave.

III · La Maison

The house
that works
at night

9e arrondissement, Paris
Ouvert la nuit — fermé le jour

Maison Éclat keeps one atelier, one door, and no daytime hours. Since 1992 we have composed only between dusk and first light, when the streets go quiet and scent behaves differently — closer to the skin, slower to speak, more honest about what it intends to leave behind.

We release one fragrance at a time, in editions of nine hundred flacons, and we do not repeat ourselves. Fumée Nº 1 took eleven years. It is not a perfume about fire. It is a perfume about what fire leaves behind — the silver hour after the flame, when a room remembers being warm.

IV · Le Rituel

How it is worn

Fumée Nº 1 is worn, not applied. Four instructions, unchanged since the first edition.

  1. 01

    Wait for dusk. Fumée does not open in daylight; its first hour belongs to the evening.

  2. 02

    One trace at the throat. One at the wrist you gesture with.

  3. 03

    Do not rub. Smoke will not be hurried, and neither should you.

  4. 04

    Then leave. The room should spend the rest of the night wondering who you were.

V · L'Attente

The ledger

The first edition of Fumée Nº 1 — nine hundred flacons — is spoken for. The second is aging now in the dark of the atelier, and will be ready when it is ready. We keep a ledger, not a queue: leave an address, and when the wax is cool the atelier will write to you. Ink, paper, no urgency.
