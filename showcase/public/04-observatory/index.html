PLATE 04 · FULL FIELD

Every star on this page was placed by arithmetic.

No photographs. No textures. No telescope, in the end, except the one you are holding — the atlas was computed the moment you arrived, and it will be computed again, identically, for whoever comes after you.

LYRA is a survey of skies that could exist — a proof that wonder does not require a lens, only a coordinate system and taste.

The Instrument

Six thousand instanced stars with black-body colour temperatures, four procedural deep-sky objects, and a camera on a spline. Rendered live in WebGL at sixty frames a second on the machine you are using right now.

The Method

Scroll position is the telescope drive. Your progress down the page slews the camera through a Catmull-Rom path between catalogue stops; nothing is filmed, nothing is faked, nothing is fetched.

The Catalogue

Four objects, honestly invented: an emission nebula, a globular cluster, a reflection nebula, a supernova remnant. Their spectra are generated per object from seeded emission lines. Hover one and read the light.
