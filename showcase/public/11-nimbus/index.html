Field Notes

From the bureau's ledger.

Entry 041 · March

A client asked for the smell of rain without the rain. We declined. Some weathers cannot be separated from their consequences; this is the first thing the bureau teaches, and the last thing clients believe.

Entry 057 · April

Delivered the fog to the reading room. The librarian stood in it for a long while, then said, "it is exactly like the morning I decided to stay." We do not know which morning she meant. We invoiced accordingly.

Entry 062 · April

Note to apprentices: when fitting rain to a bedroom, measure the roof first. The same rain is a lullaby on slate and an argument on tin. Weather is acoustics wearing a coat.

The Instruments

How a weather is taken in.

The Sitting

You describe the afternoon you want to live inside. We take measurements: ceiling height, light direction, the sound your windows make. Two visits, one of them unannounced.

The Draft

A scale model of your weather runs in the atelier for a week. You may visit it. Most clients ask for less lightning than they originally wanted; all of them ask for more dusk.

The Fitting

Installation takes a day. Calibration takes a season — rain must learn a room the way a coat learns shoulders. Adjustments are free for the life of the weather.
