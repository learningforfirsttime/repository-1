AERARIUM·VELL PUBLIC ACCOUNTS OF A CITY THAT FLOATS · FILED FROM 2,300 M FOLIO 312

Annual Report · Year 312 of the Ascent

The city held its altitude, its temper, and most of its umbrellas.

Herein the Treasury of Vell renders its yearly account: where the city stood in the sky, what the clouds paid in rain, who arrived on the winds, and how the barrels were spent. Compiled by the Office of the Aerarium; every figure drawn live, in code, before the reader.

FIG. 1

We drifted up eleven metres, on average, politely.

Mean altitude held by the city each month. The March Gale cost us forty metres and one bandstand; the Festival of Lanterns required releasing ballast, as it does every year, over the objections of the Treasurer.

FIG. 2

The year in four numbers.

FIG. 3

The clouds paid 1.2 million barrels, mostly in autumn.

Rain harvested from the city's collection sails, by month. Petal area tracks the take; October alone filled a fifth of the cisterns. Values in thousands of barrels; the full ledger is beneath the figure.

FIG. 4

Forty-eight thousand birds now outnumber us two to one.

Registered citizens against naturalised birds, by month. The Registry reminds readers that a bird is naturalised the third time it returns; the birds do not acknowledge the Registry.

FIG. 5

The wind arrived, as usual, from the southwest.

Hours of wind by compass bearing. The city is moored nose-to-southwest for this reason; visitors who insist the streets "all lean" are correct, and thanked for their observation.

FIG. 6

Where the barrels went.

Treasury flows for Year 312, in thousands of barrels. Rain tax remains our great income; lightning insurance remains our great regret. Flows are proportional; hover any band for the figure.

“In my opinion the accounts are true, fair, and lighter than air. I note, as every year, that the city keeps no reserve of gravity whatsoever, and, as every year, that it does not seem to need one.”
