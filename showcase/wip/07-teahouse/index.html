A tea house in the old style

Eight seats, one room, and a kettle that has not gone cold in a hundred years. We take guests by reservation, season by season, and answer every letter in ink.

季節

The room, four ways

The four seasons of the room

The room is nine feet square and it never changes; only the light does. Each season the host alters one scroll and one flower, and nothing else. Sit long enough and you will notice what the room already knows.

和敬清寂

Two hours, five gestures

The ceremony

A sitting lasts near two hours and follows a path worn smooth by four hundred years of hands. You do not need to know the steps. The room will show you.

  1. i

    The stones

    Seven stones cross the garden. Whatever followed you through the city cannot follow you across them.

  2. ii

    The low door

    The doorway stands three feet high. Emperor or merchant, every guest bows to enter — and enters equal.

  3. iii

    The kettle

    Well water, brought just short of a boil. Listen — the simmer is called matsukaze, wind in the pines.

  4. iv

    The whisk

    Two scoops of matcha, whisked until the surface settles into still green foam. The host's wrist does the remembering.

  5. v

    The bowl

    Turned twice in the hand, offered with its best face toward you. Drink slowly. Say nothing you do not mean.

A seat, in season

We seat eight guests at each sitting and keep two places for chance. Tell us your month and your number; the host reads requests each evening by lamplight, and replies in his own hand.
