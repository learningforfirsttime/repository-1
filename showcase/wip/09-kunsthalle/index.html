Kunsthalle Nord has no collection, no storage and no habit. Each season the building is rehung wall by wall — six exhibitions across four halls, September to June, each one a new argument about what an image owes its viewer.

The poster machine. The season identity is not a picture but a program: circles, bars and one diagonal redraw themselves into a new balanced composition every few seconds, always on the same twelve-column grid as this page. Click the poster — or the red square — to set it yourself.

    Nº 01 · Halle 1 · 12.09.26 – 23.11.26

    Retina Studies — Mira Vogel

    “The eye is not a camera. It is an archive with a temper.”

    — Mira Vogel, studio conversation, March 2026

    For nine years Vogel has painted in a room without windows. She stares at a lit form until it is switched off, then paints only what the retina holds — the stain, the drift, the slow negotiation between violet and green as the afterimage decays. The forty panels in Halle 1 are hung in the order they were seen, at the exact height of the artist’s eye.

    We show them in falling light. From opening hour to closing the hall dims by imperceptible degrees, so that the last visitors of the day see the paintings roughly as Vogel first did: barely, and therefore completely.
